What is Cricket?

Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players. One team bats to score runs, while the other team bowls and fields to stop them. Players score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets, or by hitting boundaries. There are three main formats of cricket: Test matches, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 (T20).

Test Cricket

Test cricket is the longest and oldest format of the game. It is played between two national teams over five days, with each team batting twice (two innings). The format tests a team's skill and strategy. Unlike shorter formats, Test matches can end in a win, loss, draw, or tie. They are played using a red ball, and players wear white uniforms.

One Day Cricket (ODI)

ODI stands for One Day International, a limited-overs format of cricket played between two national teams. Each team gets to bat for a maximum of 50 overs. The team that scores more runs wins the game. ODIs are known for being fast-paced and exciting, played with a white ball and colorful uniforms.
